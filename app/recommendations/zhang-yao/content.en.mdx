Zhang Yao is a senior investor whose method is especially useful for individual investors. The following ten points distill years of his experience.

1. Any company held as a major position must be inside your circle of competence.
2. Set reasonable return expectations—typically 15% annualized, or a doubling every five years.
3. Do not let price moves in companies outside your competence influence your emotions or trades.
4. Do not make prediction the starting point. Prepare a response for each plausible outcome.
5. When markets are depressed and a company is undervalued, own as many shares as possible. As markets become euphoric, reduce gradually.
6. Valuation matters. Even a wonderful company must be reasonably priced. Avoid high valuations, insist on a margin of safety, and minimize permanent loss.
7. Follow the macroeconomy appropriately, but do not let macro fluctuations determine decisions about a target company.
8. Study industries and companies with a long horizon, focusing on competitive advantage.
9. Treat shares owned multiplied by earnings per share as your profit, and dividends received as your cash flow—think like an owner.
10. Make decisions as simple and relaxed as possible. Invest only in familiar companies whose quality you can judge quickly; choosing one or two companies every one or two years is enough.
